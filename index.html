Introduction

OpenCoF studies Chain-of-Frame reasoning, where a video generation model reasons through temporally connected frames rather than only through text or static visual evidence.

We introduce OpenCoF-17K, a reasoning video dataset spanning 11 task families, and Wan-CoF, a Wan2.2-I2V-A14B model fine-tuned with diverse temporal supervision. We further explore visual reasoning tokens and textual reasoning tokens as complementary ways to organize intermediate reasoning state.

OpenCoF-17K

OpenCoF-17K contains 17,312 samples across 11 task families. Each instance pairs an initial conditioning image and text prompt with a target reasoning video standardized to 480p, 15 fps, and 81 frames.

Exploration of CoF Techniques

Wan-CoF is obtained by LoRA fine-tuning Wan2.2-I2V-A14B on OpenCoF-17K. Building on this data-only stage, we study visual reasoning tokens inserted into the visual latent sequence and textual reasoning tokens added to the text-conditioning sequence.

Evaluation on Video Reasoning Benchmarks

Wan-CoF improves the Wan2.2-I2V-A14B baseline on four external video reasoning benchmarks. Reasoning-token variants further improve the headline metrics, with vt leading on Gen-ViRe and RULER-Bench, and tt leading on MME-CoF and VIPER.
